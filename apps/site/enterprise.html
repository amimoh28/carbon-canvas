The Carbon Canvas gap

A focused layer between usage tools and carbon accounting.

Carbon Canvas is designed to connect AI-use awareness and environmental education across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and other browser-based tools. It produces estimates, not provider telemetry, and explains the uncertainty behind every range.

The practical goal is a defensible learning and decision-support program: help a voluntary cohort understand its usage patterns, compare scenarios, and identify opportunities for more intentional AI use.

Important boundary

Carbon Canvas is not a replacement for security monitoring, AI governance, lifecycle assessment, supplier emissions data, or reporting-grade corporate carbon accounting. Pilot outputs should not be presented as audited emissions.

Enterprise readiness

What a mature company deployment will require.

We are building toward these requirements rather than implying they are already complete. Each design-partner review will help determine the right order.

Product controls

Reliable extension deployment, aggregate dashboards, exports, role-based access, methodology versioning, accessibility, and support commitments.

Security and privacy

Documented data flows, encryption and deletion controls, a data-processing agreement, subprocessor disclosure, incident procedures, and institutional review.

Evidence and outcomes

Completed pilots, clearly defined success measures, final findings, recommended actions, and limitations that decision-makers can defend.
